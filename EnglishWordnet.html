What is English Word Net?

Japanese Wordnet is a Japanese concept dictionary. Each individual concept is grouped into a unit called "synset", and they are semantically linked to other synsets. This dictionary was built based on the English word net.

About search

You can try it from"WordNet Search"on the left frame .

Search from Japanese or English

select a language in "Language" and press "Search WN". Click the link for the corresponding synset offset from the search results to display the synset contents.
Note) When searching from English, uppercase letters will not be hit, so be sure to enter all lowercase letters even when searching for proper names.

Search from synset offset (synset number, described later)

No language selection required. Enter the number in the "Look up Word (or Synset)" window and press "Search WN".

1 synset number (synset offset)

It is represented by "8 digit number + "-(half-width hyphen)" + alphabet. The alphabet represents part of speech,

  • n= noun
  • v= verb
  • a= adjective
  • r= adverb

Is represented. A more specific explanation of part of speech will be given in this section. Hereinafter, "8-digit number + "-(half-width hyphen)" + alphabet" is all synset number.

2 Synonyms (synonym)

A word used to describe the concept. The English version was created using the English Wordnet, and the Japanese version was created using multiple other language Wordnets and dictionaries for Japan. After that, we manually corrected it to some extent, but there are still some that have not been checked, and there are cases where errors are included or things that should be included are not included.Click here please let us know.

3 definition sentence/example sentence (gloss)

The English text is from the English word net, and the Japanese is translated by us. When translating, I avoided translation as much as possible so that the meaning of the synset could be communicated directly. Concepts that are unfamiliar to jargon or Japanese speakers are difficult to translate, and may contain some errors. Also, since the focus was on attaching Japanese translations to all synsets, it seems that there are still other incorrect translations in comparison with mistranslations and related synsets. If you know of any such reason, come here please let us know.

Actual synonyms

About selection
When I checked it manually, I chose a word that could express the concept as accurately as possible. Therefore, Japanese synonyms may differ from the general translation of English synonyms. For example, "seal" of marine animals is generally translated as "seal", but it is correct "generic name for seals and sea lions", so "seal" (02076196-n) is synonymous with Is not registered. "Seal" matches the meaning of the subordinate concept "earless seal" (02079389-n), so it is registered as a synonym here. However, the actual synset may include not only the words that fit the definition properly, but also the subordinate concepts. For example, "Western dog" included in the synonym of "dog" (02084071-n). This is a tentative measure because no matching synset currently exists, and we plan to deal with it sequentially, such as creating a new synset.
Honorific/Slang
Honorifics and slang are handled differently for each synset.
Normally, "mother" (10332385-n) is treated as a synonym for the same synset, as the synonym "mother" and its respected word "mother" are included, but "marihuana"( 02949691-n) "Marijuana" and its common name "weed" (03990834-n) "grass" may be separate synsets.
This difference in handling comes from English WordNet (the definition of synset of English WordNet and the relationship between synsets are not modified), and if it corresponds to this, it will be done in step with English WordNet. I think it will happen.
About notation
Basically, parentheses and symbols are not used for the synonyms of Japanese word net (except "-" added to the prefix).
This is also because it was intended to be read by a computer, but I think that basically anything that does not match the concept without parentheses is inappropriate as a synonym. In addition, we are not currently able to deal with the difference in notation.
For example, "pull out", "pull out", "pull out" and "pull out" are treated as completely separate words. This is also going to be organized.
However, the correspondence regarding "reading" is undecided, and it is not specified whether "stop" is "stop" or "stop".
For each part of speech
n=noun
Words that have both nouns and adjective parts of speech in a general Japanese dictionary are basically not registered in the n=noun synset.
For example, in the case of "poor badness", the expression "extremely bad" can be expressed as a noun, but the essence is an adjective and is limited to several expressions such as "extremely XX". Therefore, I registered only in the a=adjective synset, and instead registered "roughness" in the n=noun synset.
v=verb
It is registered in the final form, or (when the syllable is used alone) with the word "do".
I avoided verb phrases as much as possible, but in some cases they are registered as synonyms if they are unavoidable for expressing the concept.
For example, "snoring" for "snore" (00017031-v) is expressed in English as "snore", but in Japanese it is only "snoring" no matter how concise. I registered because I can't help it.

There are cases where there is a real difference in meaning, like the relationship between "seal" and "seal" mentioned above.
